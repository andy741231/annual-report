UH Day of Remembrance

The UH Day of Remembrance is historically held the first Tuesday in March each year to honor our Cougar Family – students, faculty, staff, alumni and friends of the University – that have passed away the previous year. During the service, four candles are lit by the respective leaders of the Student Government Association, Faculty Senate, Staff Council, and the UH Alumni Association. At the conclusion of the ceremony, 27 chimes sound in tribute. The UH Day of Remembrance is presented by the Campus Ministries Association and the A.D. Bruce Religion Center. Support is also provided by the Division of Student Affairs and Enrollment Services, Student Centers, Student Government Association, the UH Alumni Association, Office of Development, Human Resources, and the Office of the Dean of Students.

Class Ring Ceremony

The Class Ring is a time-honored tradition recognized by Cougars worldwide. It represents the UH experience, acknowledging hard work and academic achievement. Each semester, the UH Alumni Association holds the Class Ring Ceremony, allowing students to participate with friends and family. As tradition dictates, all class rings spend the night before the Ring Ceremony at the Houston Zoo, guarded by Shasta, before being handed out to the lucky recipients.

Forever In Our Hearts

The Zoo said goodbye to its former cougars, Shasta VI and Haley in August 2022 as the two elderly felines were euthanized several months apart for declining health. Both animals had spinal and kidney illnesses which is common in older cats. While the animal health and care teams worked tirelessly to treat both cougars, it was determined that their wellbeing would not improve. The Houston Zoo is committed to providing the best-possible welfare for the animals in their care from birth to death. Haley and Shasta VI will be remembered while the new additions are celebrated.

New Beginnings

As the year 2022 was nearing its end, the University of Houston announced the long-awaited news that it was going to once again have a live cougar mascot to carry on the Cougar spirit throughout the UH community and beyond. After a rancher found two orphaned cubs on his property, the Washington State Fish & Wildlife Services contacted the Houston Zoo. Shortly thereafter, the two cubs were transported to the Lone Star state where they settled into their new habitat at the Houston Zoo. Once they become acclimated to their new surroundings, the University of Houston will work with its steadfast partners at the Houston Zoo to arrange a time where alumni and friends can meet Shasta VII and his brother, Louie in-person.
